The Philosophy

Identity unlocks creativity.
Creativity in motion develops leadership.

"Identity unlocks creativity. Creativity is not just the art you make — it is also the act of becoming. When you know who you are, you discover what you were made to create. There is an art to discovering your passion and developing it into the work you do — that is the becoming. Creativity, put in motion, develops leadership. Movement is the direction your life takes when identity is activated. Leadership is not a title. It is the fruit of the character born from your identity, your growth, and your development. When you fully know yourself, you cannot help but lead."

The Creative Movement · Core Belief

Emerge: Core is where that journey begins. Before a young person can lead anything — a team, a community, a creative practice — they need to know who they are. That is the work of this program. Nine months. Eighteen sessions. One question at the center of everything: Who are you, really?

When & Where

After school · August through May

Bi-weekly sessions of 90 minutes each, held at or near the partner school. Deliberately after school so participation doesn't require pulling students from class time. The Showcase takes place in early May.

Who Can Join

Ages 13–18 · Application required · Limited spaces

Spaces are limited. Students apply, and those accepted receive a formal invitation into the program. There is no audition, no GPA requirement, and no previous arts experience needed. The only requirements are showing up and being present and engaged in the process.

Session Format

Teaching · Conversation · Creative Workshop

Every session follows the same rhythm: a teaching moment, honest group conversation, a creative activity, and a closing takeaway. Something students carry into their week — not just their notebook.

School Partnership

We come to them

Emerge runs through a school partnership model — Love Gives provides the facilitator, curriculum, and materials. The school provides space and a point of contact. The school doesn't run the program. We do.

Cost & Scholarships

$500 per student · Full scholarships available

The program fee is $500 per student for the full year — covering two cohort leaders and all materials. Full scholarships are available and cover the cost completely. No student is turned away for financial reasons. Contact us to learn about scholarship availability.

What parents & students want to know

What does my student actually do in this program?
They attend 18 sessions across the school year — two per month, 90 minutes each, after school from August through May. Every session includes a teaching moment, honest group conversation, a creative activity, and something they take home. The creative medium varies: some months it's writing, some months visual art, some months performance or design. The constant is the question underneath: who are you, and what are you made of?
How does my student get into the program?
Spaces are limited, so students apply. Those who are accepted receive a formal invitation into the program. There is no audition, no GPA requirement, and no previous arts experience needed. The only requirements are showing up and being present and genuinely engaged in the process.
What does it cost?
The program fee is $500 per student for the full year, which covers two cohort leaders and all materials. Full scholarships are available and cover the cost completely — no student is turned away because of finances. Contact us to ask about scholarship availability.
How are young men and young women handled differently?
The curriculum, the six domains, and the Showcase are the same for everyone. What differs is how sessions are facilitated — the conversation style, the examples used, and the way the community is built. Cohorts may be separated by gender for sessions depending on school setup, with both groups coming together for the Showcase in June.
What does the year end with?
The Creative Movement Showcase in June — a public event where every student presents their creative project and personal statement to family, school community, and program supporters. It is one of the most powerful evenings of the year.
Is there a Year 2?
Yes. Students who complete Emerge: Core are eligible for Emerge: Impact — the second year, focused on career exploration, leadership in action, and building a real portfolio. Core is the foundation. Impact is where students put it in motion.

What donors & funders want to know

How is the program funded?
Emerge: Core runs on a $500 per-student program fee that covers two cohort leaders and all materials for the year. Full scholarships are available and cover the cost completely — no student is turned away for financial reasons. Additional funding comes from individual donors, foundation grants, and a percentage of revenue from All En Vision's for-profit ecosystem.
What does sponsoring a student mean?
A $500 sponsorship covers one student's full program fee — 18 sessions, all materials, facilitator cost, and their place at the Showcase in early May. It is a named, tangible investment: one student, one year, witnessed from start to finish. Sponsors who attend the Showcase see exactly who they invested in.
How do I know it's working?
The Showcase is the evidence. Donors who attend the early May event see — in a single evening — who these students were in August and who they are now. It is not a report. It is a room full of young people who know their own names.
Is there a longer-term impact story?
Yes — and this is what makes Emerge different. Core is Year 1. Year 2 is Emerge: Impact, where students apply their identity to real career and leadership development. Alumni from both years feed into a growing community — and eventually into the adult Thrive Circle through All En Vision. A student sponsored at 14 can become a Thrive Circle member at 24. That is a longitudinal story of transformation very few programs can tell.

What school administrators want to know

What does a school partnership actually require from us?
Very little. Love Gives runs the program — we provide the facilitator, the curriculum, and all materials. The school provides access to students through recruitment, a space to hold sessions after school, and a point of contact for communication. We do not require pulling students from class time, teacher participation, or curriculum integration.
How are students recruited and selected?
Counselors, teachers, or administrators identify students who would benefit and encourage them to apply. Spaces are limited — accepted students receive a formal invitation. There is no audition, no GPA requirement, and no previous arts experience needed. The only requirements are presence and genuine engagement.
What does it cost the school or families?
The program fee is $500 per student for the year, covering two cohort leaders and all materials. Full scholarships are available and cover the cost completely. The school itself incurs no cost — fees are handled between families and Love Gives directly. No student is turned away for financial reasons.
How is gender handled within the program?
The curriculum and six development domains are the same for all students. Facilitation style and session community may be organized by gender depending on the school's setup and student population — same material, delivered in the way that best serves each group. Both cohorts unite for the Showcase in June.
What does the school get out of this?
A 9-month development experience for a cohort of students that costs the school nothing — program fees are covered by student tuition and donor sponsorships. Students who complete the program demonstrate measurably stronger self-awareness, communication, and community engagement. Several schools have noted the Showcase as one of one of the most meaningful evenings of the school year.
Is there a Year 2 available for students who complete Core?
Yes. Emerge: Impact is the second-year program — designed for students who have completed Core and are ready to apply their identity work to career exploration, cohort leadership roles, and a real portfolio. Schools can host both years simultaneously once the program is established.
